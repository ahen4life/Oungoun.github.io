Role of locating the decoupling point in a supply chain as part of mass production

This is what could be done after an internship in a very new 4.0 plant and a research master in Decision Sciences and Risk Management

Posted by Ben on August 24, 2017

Abstract

Supply Chain Management is now a strategic pole for organizations. Manufacturers must be able to produce faster, better and tailor-made.

The notion of decoupling point corresponds to the gap between the high-flow production system and the pulled-flow production system. It can also be defined as the separation between "Lean" production and "Agile" production. Thus, the two types of production framing this strategic barrier have their pros and cons. In this study, a simulation method is proposed. Using the software R, a linear production chain is simulated. Modifications in the location of the decoupling point are studied to confirm the strategic role of this location talking about cost of stock holding and service rate . As a result, two conclusions are debated: the total value of stocks increases when the decoupling point is moved upstream of the production chain and the service rate tends to fall when the decoupling point is located too far downstream . In conclusion, the importance of new practices in industry 4.0 is highlighted and different possibilities are given to improve the model.
